# Focus shift: pause monetization and prioritize audience, trust, and content
Pause revenue conversations and concentrate on building a reliable product that attracts repeat local users. Early priorities should be audience growth, editorial quality, and community relationships—those create leverage for monetization later. Treat the first 6–12 months as product-market fit work: test formats, measure engagement, and iterate quickly.

---

## Core objectives for the next 90 days
- Establish a clear editorial identity — tone, beats, and publishing cadence.  
- Build distribution channels — newsletter, social profiles, YouTube channel, and local partnerships.  
- Create repeatable content workflows — templates for shorts, features, and event roundups.  
- Grow a contributor network — students, freelancers, business owners, and civic partners.  
- Measure engagement, not revenue — time on page, repeat visitors, newsletter open rate, and event submissions.

---

## Practical content and product actions
- Launch week lineup — publish a Foreword, 1 Downtown feature, 1 UNC piece, 1 West 10th short, and a "Welcome New Residents" checklist.  
- Video-first push — produce three 60–90s neighborhood shorts and one 3–4 minute hub video to anchor the Video Collection.  
- Newsletter playbook — create a weekly digest template with 3 local stories, 1 event, and 1 job/gig highlight.  
- Community calendar — embed a living events calendar and invite venue managers to submit listings.  
- Contributor onboarding — one‑page guidelines, release form, and a simple pitch form.

---

## Distribution and community engagement tactics
- Cross-post vertical clips to TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts with local hashtags and venue tags.  
- Partner with UNC student media for co-produced segments and internship pipelines.  
- Host a launch meetup downtown or at a West 10th business to introduce the brand and collect UGC.  
- Create a "Viewer Clip" slot each week to encourage submissions and build local ownership.  
- Moderate comments and local forums to surface story ideas and build trust.

---

## Non-monetary KPIs to track now
- Newsletter signups — baseline and weekly growth.  
- Repeat visitors — percentage of returning users within 30 days.  
- Video completion rate — for 60–90s shorts and 3–5 minute features.  
- Event submissions — number of user-submitted events per week.  
- Contributor pipeline — active contributors and published pieces per month.

---

## Quick operational checklist (first 2 weeks)
- Finalize launch editorial calendar for 14 days.  
- Produce and publish the hub video + three neighborhood shorts.  
- Set up newsletter template and first automated send.  
- Create contributor guidelines and a simple submission form.  
- Schedule the launch meetup and invite 8–12 local partners.
