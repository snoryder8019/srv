# Media Plan: Visual Asset Collection Strategy

## Geographic Focus
- **UNC Campus** — student life, campus events, academic spaces
- **Downtown Chapel Hill** — restaurants, shops, galleries, street scenes, events
- **West 10th Corridor** — local businesses, storefronts, community hubs
- **West Greeley** — residential character, parks, neighborhood life

---

## Collection Priorities by Format

### Video Assets (Primary)
- **60–90 second neighborhood shorts** — one per location zone; showcase local flavor, foot traffic, business energy
- **3–5 minute feature videos** — deep dives on a business, event, or community story
- **Hub/anchor video (3–4 min)** — "Welcome to Chapel Hill" or "This is Your Downtown" establishing piece
- **Event coverage** — live captures from UNC events, downtown gatherings, street fairs
- **Testimonial/founder clips** — 30–45 sec soundbites from local business owners and community leaders

### Photo Assets (Supporting)
- **Hero images** — high-quality establishing shots of each neighborhood zone (1–2 per location)
- **Business portraits** — storefront shots, interior ambiance, signature products/services
- **Event coverage** — crowd shots, moment captures, details (food, signage, people)
- **Candid/lifestyle** — students, shoppers, workers; authentic local moments
- **Grid/mood tiles** — vertical formats optimized for Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest

---

## Shooting Specifications

### Video
- **Device:** iPhone 15 Pro (or better) or mirrorless camera with stabilization  
- **Frame rate:** 24–30fps; 60fps for slow-mo moments  
- **Resolution:** 4K (3840×2160) when possible; minimum 1080p  
- **Audio:** Lavalier mic for interviews; ambient audio capture for B-roll  
- **Vertical formats:** Produce at 9:16 for TikTok/Reels; export 16:9 and 9:16 variants  

### Photography
- **Resolution:** Minimum 3000×2000px (allows flexibility for web/print/social)  
- **Color profile:** sRGB for web; shoot in RAW for post-processing flexibility  
- **Lighting:** Golden hour preferred (sunrise/sunset); cloud cover for even light  
- **Compositions:** Wide establishing, medium detail shots, close-up product/food details  

---

## Collection Schedule (First 8 Weeks)

### Week 1–2: Scouting & Logistics
- Identify 8–10 key locations per zone (24–40 total)  
- Reach out to business owners for permission and availability windows  
- Scout times of day with best light and foot traffic  
- Secure equipment (tripod, lav mic, external drives)  

### Week 3–4: UNC Campus & Downtown Blitz
- 2–3 half-day shoots at UNC (classrooms, quad, library, dining)  
- 2–3 half-day shoots Downtown (storefronts, cafes, galleries, street scenes)  
- Collect 40–60 video clips + 80–120 still images per zone  

### Week 5–6: West 10th & West Greeley
- 2–3 half-day shoots on West 10th (businesses, street views, evening energy)  
- 2–3 half-day shoots in West Greeley (parks, residential, family moments)  
- Collect 40–60 video clips + 80–120 still images per zone  

### Week 7–8: Event Coverage & Interviews
- Capture 2–3 local events (opening, market, festival, class, meetup)  
- Record 4–6 founder/community leader testimonials (10–15 min each; extract 30–60 sec clips)  
- Fill gaps and re-shoot underperforming sequences

- [ ] Signed location permissions (email or form)
- [ ] Owner/participant verbal OK for filming
- [ ] Release form for interviews/testimonials
- [ ] Wide/medium/close-up coverage per location
- [ ] B-roll (activity, product, atmosphere, crowd)
- [ ] Golden hour / optimal lighting window hit
- [ ] Audio recording (interviews, ambient)
- [ ] At least 3 takes of critical moments
- [ ] Metadata logged (location, date, subject, keywords)

```
    ├── shot-log.csv
    ├── rights-releases.pdf
    └── keywords.md
```

### Metadata Tracking
- **Shot Log:** location, date, time, subject, creator, keyword tags
- **Rights:** which assets have release forms, who owns the rights
- **Usage:** which clips are in which published pieces

---

## Rights & Permissions

- **Location permission form** — one-pager for business owners (email + on-site)  
- **Photo/video release** — for interviews, testimonials, identifiable people  
- **Attribution standard** — credit local partners in video description/credits  
- **Storage:** releases + permissions in `/media/metadata/rights-releases.pdf`  

---

## Delivery & Repurposing

### Short-Term (Weeks 1–4 of launch)
- Extract 30–45s clips from longer videos for social teaser posts  
- Publish one 60–90s neighborhood short per week  
- Use stills as blog feature images and newsletter headers  

### Mid-Term (Months 2–3)
- Compile 3–4 minute feature videos from location footage  
- Recut testimonials into "Meet Your Downtown" series  
- Build seasonal/event-driven compilations  

### Long-Term (6–12 months)
- Build a searchable asset library for contributors and partners  
- Reuse best-performing clips in year-end recaps  
- License high-quality stills to local publications
